What is a cognitive function stack?

There are eight Jungian cognitive functions. Your MBTI type arranges four as the primary stack and four as the shadow stack (positions 1 to 8). That arrangement is your cognitive function stack.

Primary and shadow stacks

Positions 1–4

Primary stack

Your conscious four, from everyday default to under-pressure rawness.

1

Dominant

First in line — your default lens; what you reach for before you think.

2

Auxiliary

Second in line — supports the dominant; balances inner life with the outside world.

3

Tertiary

Third in line — familiar enough to lean on; easy to overuse or loop on.

4

Inferior

Fourth in line — least practiced; surfaces under stress, often exaggerated or clumsy.

Positions 5–8

Shadow stack

The four not in your primary order. Less integrated, often louder under stress, conflict, or projection.

5

Opposing

Fifth in line — pushes against the dominant; resistance, contradiction, stubborn friction.

6

Critical parent

Sixth in line — the nagging inner judge; rigid, harsh, or dismissive when it flares.

7

Trickster

Seventh in line — blind spots and mischief; slippery logic, absurdity, or getting played.

8

Demon

Eighth in line — the hardest edge; deepest stress mode, often all-or-nothing.

How Types Connect

Relationship Compatibility

Cognitive functions shape how types relate. Some pairs flow easily — others create growth through friction.

NF — The Idealists

NF types connect through shared values and meaning. Friendships form fast and run deep — sometimes too intense for other temperaments.

INFP INFJ ENFP ENFJ

Friendship vibe: easiest with NF + NF (same emotional shorthand) · energizing contrast with SP (play and presence without heavy processing)

NT — The Analysts

NT friendships are built on ideas and mutual respect for competence. They love a good debate and don't need constant contact to maintain closeness.

INTP INTJ ENTP ENTJ

Friendship vibe: low-maintenance with NT + NT (pick up where you left off) · warmth without clinginess with NF (if you make room for feelings)

SJ — The Guardians

SJ friendships are steady and dependable. They show care through action — remembering details, showing up, following through. Loyalty is everything.

ISFJ ISTJ ESFJ ESTJ

Friendship vibe: rock-solid with SJ + SJ (shared rituals) · loosened up with SP (fun breaks the routine)

SP — The Explorers

SP friendships are energetic and present. They bond through doing things together — adventure, craft, experience. They don't overthink relationships.

ISFP ISTP ESFP ESTP

Friendship vibe: kinetic with SP + SP (always something to do) · grounding with SJ (someone who actually texts back and shows up)

NF — The Idealists

At work, NFs light up when mission and people align — but several NFs in the same room can echo the same tradeoff avoidance (alignment without closure). Cross-temperament partners (especially NT for rigor or SJ for execution) often cover gaps an all-idealist lineup misses. Purely metrics-only cultures still feel hollow to them.

INFP INFJ ENFP ENFJ

At work: pair NF + NT or NF + SJ for clearer roles · stack NF + NF carefully (two visionaries, one calendar)

NT — The Analysts

NTs excel at strategy, systems, and debate. They respect competence and autonomy — and do best when meetings have a point, not just rapport for its own sake.

INTP INTJ ENTP ENTJ

At work: clean handoffs with SJ (your idea, their checklist) · tension with another NT when two strategists debate instead of shipping

SJ — The Guardians

SJs are the steady operators — planning, standards, and accountability. They keep commitments real and processes running; friction hits when rules shift without good reason.

ISFJ ISTJ ESFJ ESTJ

At work: field + ops synergy with SP (real-time fixes, your playbook) · pushback from NT if “process” blocks a needed pivot

SP — The Explorers

SPs shine hands-on — crisis response, craft, sales floors, anything that rewards reading the room and moving fast. They chafe when hierarchy blocks initiative.

ISFP ISTP ESFP ESTP

At work: fast iteration with NT (options on the fly) · missed handoffs with another SP unless someone owns the calendar

NF — The Idealists

In love, NFs chase depth — emotional attunement, shared meaning, and a sense of being truly known. Intensity can feel magical or overwhelming, depending on the partner.

INFP INFJ ENFP ENFJ

In romance: deep recognition with NF + NF (watch idealizing) · growth arc with NT (clarity vs emotional weather — negotiate pace and reassurance)

NT — The Analysts

NTs value respect, intellectual chemistry, and room to breathe. Growth often means naming feelings without turning every emotion into a problem to solve.

INTP INTJ ENTP ENTJ

In romance: spark with NF (mind meets heart) · steadiness with SJ (plans you can trust) · watch alone-time vs closeness

SJ — The Guardians

SJs show love through commitment — plans kept, problems handled, people looked after. They want reliability from a partner; spontaneity may take practice, not instinct.

ISFJ ISTJ ESFJ ESTJ

In romance: comfort with SP (fun breaks the grind) · tenderness with NF (words matter) · friction when “the plan” meets last-minute chaos

SP — The Explorers

SPs bond through the present — shared adventures, humor, touch, and “let’s just go.” Keeping novelty alive matters; endless routine-only weeks can feel like slow suffocation.

ISFP ISTP ESFP ESTP

In romance: chemistry with NF (depth + sensation) · grounding with SJ (someone who stays) · negotiate freedom vs future talk early
